---
title: google business profile and why local clients need it set up properly
date: 2026-05-08
category: seo
---

For a local business — a salon, a clinic, a restaurant, a consultant — Google Business Profile often drives more inbound traffic than the website itself. It's the card that appears when someone searches the business name, or when someone searches "service near me." Getting it right matters more than most technical SEO work on the site.

**what to fill in completely**

Name, address, phone number, website, category, hours, description, photos. All of it. Incomplete profiles rank lower and convert worse. The description should mention the location and primary service naturally, not as keyword stuffing.

**NAP consistency**

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone. These must match exactly across Google Business, the website footer, any directories, and anywhere else the business is listed. "Street" vs "St." vs "Ul." matters. Inconsistency confuses Google's local ranking algorithm.

**photos**

Businesses with photos get significantly more clicks than those without. For a restaurant, that's food photos. For a salon, that's the space and results. For a clinic, that's the exterior and interior so people can recognize it. Upload real photos, not stock images. Google knows the difference.

**reviews**

Respond to every review, positive and negative. For negative reviews, respond calmly and offer to resolve the issue offline. The response isn't for the reviewer — it's for everyone else reading it. A business that responds thoughtfully to criticism looks more trustworthy than one that ignores it or argues.

**posts**

Google Business has a posts feature — short updates that appear on the profile. Most businesses ignore this. Using it occasionally signals that the profile is actively managed, which helps visibility.

**the thing to verify**

After setting everything up, search for the business from a different device or in incognito mode. See what the profile actually looks like. Fix anything that looks wrong from a customer's perspective, not a developer's.

This takes two hours to set up properly. It's one of the highest-leverage things you can do for a local business client.
