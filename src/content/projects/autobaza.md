## Overview

Most vehicle listing platforms in the region have the same problem: they're either too simple or too overwhelming. Buyers can't filter effectively, sellers have limited control over how their listings appear, and there's no reason for anyone to come back after a transaction is done. Autobaza was built to fix all three.

My role was full platform development on WordPress.

## The Problem

The client wanted a marketplace that felt serious. Not a classifieds board but a proper platform where both buyers and sellers had tools that worked. Listings needed to be detailed enough to make a real decision from. Search needed to be precise enough to actually reduce the result set. And the platform needed enough engagement mechanics to keep users coming back.

## What I Built

**Vehicle Listings**

Each listing covers everything a buyer needs: make, model, year, mileage, fuel type, emission class, registration status, import country, damage assessment and price. The grid layout is optimized for scanning. When you know what you're looking for, you can find it fast.

**Search and Filtering**

A comprehensive filtering system lets buyers narrow by vehicle type, condition, engine specs, safety features, location and price range. The goal was to reduce the result set to something actually useful, not just sort a long list.

**User System**

The platform supports both private sellers and commercial dealers as separate entities with different toolsets. Buyers can filter by seller type. Features include personal listing management, a favorites and watchlist, direct messaging between buyers and sellers, notifications and full profile management.

**Rewards System**

A points-based rewards layer incentivizes engagement. Users earn points through platform activity, which creates a reason to return beyond a single transaction. A small mechanic that meaningfully changes retention behavior.
